Greatest Guitarists

Guitarists who have influenced generations, their sound has changed the world.

50. Dimebag Darrell-Before he was shot and killed on December 8th, 2004, "Dimebag" Darrell Abbott was a sensation. His ability to fret low notes with speed, and use of the floyd rose bridge, helped his band Pantera become one of the most influencial bands to modern day music. Pantera's hits "Walk" and "Cowbodys from H***" will forever be remembered as some of the greatest songs in history. Dimebag will forever be remembered as the King of 2000's metal music
49. Mike Campbell- Tom Petty's lead guitarist ,Mike Campbell, electrified Heartbreakers greatest hits. Whether it was his weeping slide guitar in "Learning to Fly", or quick licks presented in "Runnin' Down A Dream",Campbell showed the world that one can play simple and sound amazing.
48. Joe Perry- Aerosmith's lead guitarist proves that he can do almost anything, from his two step bends, to his melodic finger picking skills. Perry helped bring Aerosmith to its peaking fame.
47. The Edge- U2's lead guitarist, David "The Edge" Evans, use of the delay pedal and chorus flanger pedal effects help form U2's greatest nostalgic hits such as, "Where The Streets Have No Name", and "Pride (In The Name of Love)"
46. Ace Freely- Kiss' lead guitarist, singer, and band former. Ace's guitar skills are based off of the Em7 scale, a rare scale.
45. John Lennon- Before his assasination in 1980, John Winston Ono Lennon was one of the greatest rhythm guitarists of all time. While Lennon was controversial, his career in the Beatles and after was full of hit after hit. His love for peace helped the Beatles compose great songs.
44. Kurt Cobain- "Father of Grunge" Kurt Cobain, before his "suicide" in 1994, Kurt had started a new revolution with his band Nirvana. His simple solos, his drop tunings, had changed music in the 1990's. Some hits of his are, "Smells Like Teen Spirit", "Heart-Shaped Box", "Polly", and "About a Girl"
43. Ritchie Blackmore- Deep Purple's lead guitarist, Ritchie Blackmore, is known for writing one of the most popular riffs in history, "Smoke on The Water". His scalloped stratocaster is now one of the most popular Fender model guitars.
42. James Hetfield- Metallica's rhythm guitarist and lead vocalist has been one of the most iconic riff writers that the world has seen. "Enter Sandman"
41. Buddy Holly- "Father of Lead Guitar" Buddy Holly is known as one of the first lead guitarists, he influences greats like Clapton, George Harrison, and Jeff Beck.
40. Willie Nelson-There is no question that Willie Nelson and his guitar, Trigger, have gone through a lot together. Nelson's use of soloing on acoustic has influenced many modern pop artists.
39. John Frusciante- RHCPs lead guitarist, with his Hendrix style solos, and Clapton vibrato, his guitar helped RHCP reach its popularity in the 90's.
38. Jack White-The master of open A slide guitar, and potentially one of the best guitarists when it comes to making blues turn heavy. "Seven Nation Army"
39. Robert Johnson-Famous blues guitarists, became an influence on a majority of legends. He infamously "sold his soul to the devil" in exchange for guitar greatness.
38. Pete Townshend-The Who's legendary lead guitarist. Pioneer of the windmill strumming style. His heavy lead riffs gave The Who the perfect tone they needed.
37. Joe Walsh- Controverisially One of two lead guitarists in Eagles. Walsh may have had a big ego, but his weeping slide combined with Don Felder's quick bends makes Eagles one of the greatest bands in history.
36. Brian May- Queen's lead guitarist. His "Red Special Guitar" was handbuilt using an 18th Century mantle and a motorcycle. His sustained tone and lead riffs became the brains behind Mercury's vocals.
35. Tony Iommi- Black Sabbath's guitarist proves that everyone can succeed no matter what. On his last day of work, his hand was crushed and had to get his finger tips amputated. Iommi took advantage of that and made fake fingertips from thimbles. Ozzy Osbourne's supergroup wouldn't be the same without Iommi. "Iron Man" "Paranoid"
34. Zakk Wylde- His famous bullseye guitars and fast picking give off a perfect tone. Has replace RR in Ozzy. He plays with emotion like no other.
33. Randy Rhoads- Before Rhoads' tragic death, Rhoads had brought a new idea to the music world... Playing with speed. He was one of the fastests, most accurate, and hardcore guitarists ever.
32. Angus Young- Legendary AC/DC lead guitarist is known for his fast pull-off/hammer on licks, double bends, his SG, duck walk, and his satanic persona on stage.
31. Steve Vai- Steve and his famous Ibanez Jem guitars are known for their wham scoops, hypnotic licks, long fingers,and speed.
30. Kirk Hammett- Metallica's lead guitarist's use of the wah pedal, trem picking, ESP guitars, shred picking, and dark music, is considered some of the best combined skills.
29. Carlos Santana- His bends, vibrato, and melodic scales help create "Black Magic"
28. Billy Gibbons- His ability to hit pinch harmonics every time make his overdriven blues style guitar even more amazing. He used .007 gauge strings (VERY THIN).
27. B.B. King- One of the greatest influences for 1980's blues, him and his guitar "Lucille" create a warm, dry blues tone.
26. Chuck Berry- The first ever lead guitarist, Berry wrote "Johnny B Goode" and is considered the "influence for all influences"
25. David Gilmour- Pink Floyd's lead guitarist's psychedelic solos include perfect two step bends, and amazing vibrato.
24. Derek Trucks- One of the greatest slide guitarists, replaced Duane Allman in Allman Brothers, after Duane died. Started at 13.
23. Duane Allman- In his short career with his family band, Duane's blues corcodin bottle slide and his hammer on/pull off bend sequence brought so many people to love him.
22. Gary Clark Jr. - Up and coming Texas blues guitarist can do it all, slide, jazz, blues, TX Shuffle, and sing.
21. Keith Richards- known for his "open G' Rhythm and lead, Richards created the riffs that The Rolling Stones would make popular for generations to come.
20. Les Paul- Known for inventing MultiTrack recording and the solidbody guitar, Les Paul has over 1,000 guitar models named after him.
19. Prince "O(+>"- To sum up Prince... He's Prince.
18. Jeff Beck- His fingerpicking, use of whammy trem vibrato, and soothing riffs create a nostalgic tone.
17. Bob Dylan- He turned folk songs into a deeper, more emotional, ballad about his struggles in life.
16. Joe Bonamassa- At 12, Bonamassa opened for blues legend B.B King. His slow blues licks give listeners the feel of Joe's emotional licks.
15. Joe Satriani- His robotic guitar rig gives his music its own personality. His motiffs are spot on rhythm-wise.
14. Muddy Waters- Along with BB King, Waters' blues licks and step bends bring a tear to audience's eyes.
13. Nancy Wilson- She has broken barriers, and influenced so many women to pick up guitars.
12. Albert King- Emotional Blues, known for his lefty Flying V.
11. Tom Morello- His whammy pedal and input slide skills add a new sound to RATM that no one has heard before.
10. Slash- Slash is the master of fast fretting, vocoder, amazing riffs, and the master of beautiful hats. He gave GNR the guitar that would forever make them popular.
9. Johnny Cash- Father of Country, his blues-country style guitar helped popularize guitar in the early 1950s. His reign lasted a whole lifetime.
8. Buckethead- His identity may be secret, but his skill isn't. His use of popguitar, speed picking, killswitch, playing guitar with rubber chickens, and KFC hat, define his amazing albums' tone and diversity

6. George Harrison- The Beatles lead guitarist can show that he can play simple and complex. His abilities of riffing beautifully and having emotional solos show people that there's no such thing as a just a lead guitarist or rhythm guitarist. He formed a successful group called Traveling Wilburys. George's religious hymns in his songs amaze people. Also, his slide in standard tuning, and sitar mastery, blows people's minds.
5. Eric Clapton- Clapton's weeping two step bends, his floating vibrato, quick trills, double picking, and verse solos are iconic to music history.
4. Eddie Van Halen- EVH's use of the Floyd Rose Bridge, the divebomb, harmonics, and invention of tapping (using to hands to play a riff) are all showcased in the solo "Eruption" Eddie's trem picking is the fastest around. There is no question that EVH is one of the most skilled guitarists. His homemade Frankenstrat is currently a national treasure.
3. Stevie Ray Vaughan- SRV was known to play the hardest guitar possible. He used 13 gauge strings (heaviest made) and had a 2 inch action. Despite the hard setup, SRV was able to give the best blues tone that the world has seen. His quick emotional bends, and his use of TX Shuffle changed music for good. His ability to play behind his back is flawless. He is the greatest American Blues Guitarist.
2. Jimmy Page- Led Zeppelin's lead guitarist can do it all, play guitar with a bow, and with a drumstick. His bends, heavy riffs, and quick licks matched perfectly to Robert Plant's singing, and John Bonham's drumming.
1. Jimi Hendrix-From playing with his teeth, to lighting a guitar on fire, to his elaborate fashion, Jimi Hendrix is the greatest guitarist that history has ever seen. His fingers could glide across a fretboard with a light touch, but play notes with a hard grunge.
